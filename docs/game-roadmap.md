# Game Roadmap

The long-term vision is to turn the simulator into a full fiscal-policy strategy game.

## Full Game Mode

Players could manage the Philippine economy across multiple years while balancing:

```text
Growth
Public welfare
Debt sustainability
Inequality
Political stability
Household cost of living
Investor confidence
Public trust
```

## Possible Game Mechanics

- election cycles
- approval rating
- public trust
- corruption risk
- investor confidence
- price-stress pressure
- disaster shocks
- global oil shocks
- food supply shocks
- debt crisis risk
- credit rating changes
- opposition pressure
- media sentiment
- protest risk
- cabinet/adviser system
- policy cards
- random events

## Citizen Mode

A simplified mode for ordinary users using everyday language:

```text
Government income
Public spending
Loans and debt
Family costs
Schools and hospitals
Jobs and investment
```

## Advanced Policy Mode

A more technical mode for analysts and researchers:

- GDP deflator
- sector-level GDP
- tax buoyancy
- unemployment
- poverty rate
- real wage growth
- regional GDP
- inflation basket decomposition
- debt service ratio
- exchange-rate assumptions
- interest-rate assumptions

## Classroom / Multiplayer Mode

- shareable scenario links
- student policy challenges
- compare team choices
- budget-passing simulation
- role-based gameplay: President, Finance Secretary, DBM, BSP, Congress, Citizens

## Story Campaigns

Possible campaigns:

- Recovering after a recession
- Managing a rice price crisis
- Reducing debt after heavy borrowing
- Expanding healthcare without breaking the budget
- Building infrastructure while controlling corruption risk
- Managing OFW remittance slowdown
- Preparing for an election year
